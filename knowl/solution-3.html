Solution 1.2.3.1.

Open 10 A current source from the circuit to find \(I_{1}.\) Using Kirchhoffâ€™s voltage law in loop \(I_{1}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} 20 = 2I_{1} + 5I_{1}-5I_{2} = 7I_{1} -5I_{2} \tag{1.2.1} \end{equation}
From loop \(I_{2}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} I_{2} = 2v_{x1}\tag{1.2.2} \end{equation}
But, \(v_{x1} \) is a voltage drop at \(5 \,\Omega\) resistance.
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad v_{x1} = 5(I_{1}-I_{2}) \tag{1.2.3} \end{equation}
From eqns.(1.2.2) and (1.2.3), we have -
\begin{equation*} 5(I_{1}-I_{2}) = \frac{I_{2}}{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} or, \quad 10I_{1}-11I_{2}= 0 \tag{1.2.4} \end{equation}
On solving eqns. (1.2.1) and (1.2.4), we get -
\begin{equation*} I_{1} = \frac{220}{27} \,A; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad I_{2} = \frac{200}{27} \,A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad v_{x1} = \frac{I_{2}}{2} = \frac{200}{27\times 2} = 3.7\, V \tag{1.2.5} \end{equation}
Now, short 20 V source and leave 10 A current source active in the circuit. From loop of \(I_{1}\)
\begin{equation*} 2I_{1} + 5I_{1}-2I_{3}-5I_{2} = 0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} 7I_{1} -5I_{2} -2I_{3} = 0 \tag{1.2.6} \end{equation}
From loop of \(I_{2}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} I_{2} = 2v_{x2} \tag{1.2.7} \end{equation}
But, \(v_{x2}\) is a voltage drop at \(5 \,\Omega\) resistance.
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad v_{x2} = 5(I_{1}-I_{2}) \tag{1.2.8} \end{equation}
From loop of \(I_{3}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} I_{3} = -10 A \tag{1.2.9} \end{equation}
From eqns. (1.2.6) and (1.2.9), we have -
\begin{equation} 7I_{1} - 5I_{2} = -20 \tag{1.2.10} \end{equation}
From eqns. (1.2.7) and (1.2.8), we have -
\begin{equation} 10I_{1} - 11I_{2} = 0 \tag{1.2.11} \end{equation}
On solving eqns. (1.2.10) and (1.2.11), we get -
\begin{equation*} I_{1} = -11\,A; \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{and}\quad I_{2} = -\frac{220}{22} \,A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad v_{x2} = \frac{I_{2}}{2} =- \frac{220}{22\times 2} = -5.0\, V \tag{1.2.12} \end{equation}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} v_{x} = v_{x1}+v_{x2} = 3.7-5.0 = -1.3\,V \end{equation*}
