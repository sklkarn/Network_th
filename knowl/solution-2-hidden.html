Remove 4 V voltage source and open 2 A current source from the circuit to find \(i_{1}.\) Using Kirchhoff’s voltage law:
\begin{equation*} 3 + 0.5\,i_{1} = (2+4)\,i_{1} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad i_{1} = \frac{3}{5.5} = \frac{6}{11} \,A \end{equation*}
Remove 3 V voltage source and open 2 A current source from the circuit to find \(i_{2}. \) Using Kirchhoff’s voltage law:
\begin{equation*} 0.5\,i_{2}-4 = 2\,i_{2} + 4\,i_{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad i_{2} = -\frac{4}{5.5} = -\frac{8}{11}\, A \end{equation*}
Remove 3 V and 4 V voltage sources from the circuit to find \(i_{3}.\) From Kirchhoff’s current law:
\begin{equation*} i_{t} = 2+i_{3} \end{equation*}
From Kirchhoff’s voltage law in outer loop:
\begin{equation*} 0.5i_{3}= 2i_{3} + 4i_{t} = 2i_{3} + 4(2+i_{3}) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad i_{3} = -\frac{16}{11} \,A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad i = i_{1}+i_{2} +i_{3}= \frac{6}{11}-\frac{8}{11} -\frac{16}{11} =- \frac{18}{11} \,A \end{equation*}
