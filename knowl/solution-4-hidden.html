Short circuit 4 V voltage source in the circuit to find \(I_{1}.\) Using nodal analysis:
\begin{equation} I_{1} = I_{1}'+I_{o}' +2v_{o}' = \frac{V-1}{2} +\frac{V-0}{3} +2v_{o}' \tag{1.2.13} \end{equation}
Here V is the voltage at upper nodes.
Now, \(V=v_{o}'.\) Also,
\begin{equation*} \frac{V}{3}= \frac{V-1}{2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad V=3 \,V \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad v_{o}' =3V \tag{1.2.14} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{and},\quad I_{o}' = \frac{V-0}{3} =\frac{3}{3} =1\,A \tag{1.2.15} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{also},\quad I_{1} = 8\,A \tag{1.2.16} \end{equation}
Now short 1 V source to find \(I_{2} \text{.}\)
\begin{equation} I_{2} = I_{1}"+I_{o}" +2v_{o}" = \frac{V}{2} +\frac{V}{3} +2v_{o}' \tag{1.2.17} \end{equation}
Now, \(V=v_{o}"=4. \) Also,
\begin{equation} I_{o}" = \frac{V}{3} = \frac{4}{3}\,A \tag{1.2.18} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} I_{2} = \frac{17v_{o}"}{6} = 34/3\,A \tag{1.2.19} \end{equation}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} I_{o}=I_{o}'+I_{o}" = 1+\frac{4}{3} =\frac{7}{3}\,A \end{equation*}
